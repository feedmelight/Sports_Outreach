What we know about your world

The Problems
You're Solving Right Now

01
Your European fanbase is invisible
The Chiefs have fans in Dublin, London, Berlin, and beyond. You know they exist. You don't know who they are, where they gather, what they feel, or how to speak to them in a way that deepens allegiance rather than just broadcasting at them.
02
The soft fan is becoming real
The fastest growing segment isn't the hardcore. It's the collector — someone with a portfolio of allegiances at different temperatures. They followed Mahomes. They watched the Taylor effect. The flame is lit. Most teams have no idea how to serve them.
03
Content doesn't keep up with the signal
Your fans are generating real-time sentiment, conversation, and energy across every platform. Your content team is on a production cycle. There's a gap between what your fans are feeling now and what you're able to say to them.
04
European activations are one-and-done
NFL London games. Sponsor activations. A fan event in Dublin. They land well and then disappear. There's no system that converts a one-night experience into a permanent deepening of the relationship. The flame lights, then goes out.
"The moment a fan becomes a real one isn't accidental. It's created. A bar in Austin at 6am watching Liverpool. A full stadium that crackles. A piece of content that makes someone feel like they belong. We understand how to build those moments — because we've lived them on both sides."
Ben Leyland, EP & Technology Director, FeedMeLight · From firsthand observation, NE USA & Austin, 2025/26
